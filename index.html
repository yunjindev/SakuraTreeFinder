✿ ABOUT SAKURA TREES ✿

There is nothing quite like standing under a Sakura tree in full bloom. The petals come down like snow, everything smells faintly sweet, and for a couple of weeks a year the whole world feels a little softer. That's what this site is about.

In Japan they call it Hanami, which just means "flower viewing." People pack lunches, spread out blankets under the trees, and sit there for hours. It's been going on for over a thousand years. The idea is that the blossoms are beautiful because they don't last. Two weeks and they're gone.

These are the varieties you're most likely to come across:

Species Common Name Bloom Period
Prunus serrulataJapanese CherryLate March – April
Prunus × yedoensisYoshino CherryLate March – April
Prunus subhirtellaHigan CherryMarch – April
Prunus pendulaWeeping CherryMarch – April
Prunus sargentiiSargent CherryApril – May
